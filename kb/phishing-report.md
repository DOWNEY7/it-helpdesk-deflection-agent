# Phishing Report

## Overview
Phishing emails are fraudulent messages designed to steal credentials, install malware, or trick employees into transferring money. **Report all suspicious emails immediately.**

## How to Identify Phishing

Warning signs of a phishing email:
- Unexpected request for login credentials, passwords, or payment
- Sender address doesn't match the company displayed (e.g., `support@micros0ft.com`)
- Urgent language: "Your account will be closed in 24 hours"
- Generic greeting: "Dear Customer" instead of your name
- Suspicious links — hover over links to see the actual URL
- Unexpected attachments (.exe, .zip, .docm files)
- Requests to bypass normal processes ("please transfer funds urgently")

## Reporting a Suspicious Email

### Method 1: Report Phishing Button (Outlook)
1. Select the suspicious email (do not open any links)
2. Click **Report** in the Outlook ribbon
3. Select **Report Phishing**
4. Outlook sends the email to the Security team and removes it from your inbox

### Method 2: Forward to Security Team
Forward the suspicious email as an attachment to: **security@company.internal**
Include a brief description of why it's suspicious.

### Method 3: IT Helpdesk
If you clicked a link or entered credentials: **call IT immediately** — do not email.

## If You Clicked a Phishing Link

**Act immediately:**
1. **Disconnect from the network** (unplug ethernet / disable WiFi)
2. **Call IT Helpdesk** — do not email (email may be compromised)
3. IT will perform a password reset, MFA re-registration, and device scan
4. Do not use the device until IT has cleared it

## If You Received a Suspicious Call (Vishing)

- IT will **never** call you to ask for your password or MFA codes
- If in doubt, hang up and call IT back on the known number
- Report vishing attempts to Security at security@company.internal

## Common Phishing Themes to Watch For

| Theme | Example |
|-------|---------|
| IT alerts | "Your password is expiring — click here" |
| HR/Payroll | "Payslip ready — confirm your bank details" |
| CEO fraud | "Urgent wire transfer needed" |
| Parcel delivery | "Your parcel is held — pay a small fee" |
| M365 alerts | "Your mailbox is full — click to upgrade" |

## When to Escalate to Security (Not Just Helpdesk)
- You entered credentials into a fake login page
- You opened an attachment and your PC is behaving strangely
- You received targeted phishing referencing internal company information
- Multiple employees in your team received the same email
